## 🌟 Project Overview

This project presents a comprehensive, visual approach to understanding electromagnetic (EM) waves. Rather than relying on static diagrams, it provides live, interactive simulations that allow students to manipulate wave parameters and observe the resulting physical behaviour in real time.

All simulations are grounded in the theoretical framework established by **James Clerk Maxwell's equations (1864)**, which unified the laws of electricity, magnetism, and optics — and predicted the existence of electromagnetic waves traveling at the speed of light. 💡

Simply open the link above in any modern browser to explore the simulation. No installation or setup is required. ✅

## 🚀 Features

### 1. 🌐 3D Wave Visualization
A fully interactive three-dimensional animation showing the electric field **(E)** and magnetic field **(B)** oscillating in perpendicular planes as the wave propagates along the X-axis.

- 🖱️ Click and drag to orbit the camera freely
- 🔍 Scroll to zoom in or out
- 👁️ Toggle the E-field and B-field on or off independently
- ⏯️ Pause and resume the animation at any point

---

### 2. 〰️ EM Wave Simulator (2D)
A real-time 2D animation demonstrating the sinusoidal nature of electromagnetic waves with full user control over wave parameters.

- 🎚️ Frequency slider (0.5 – 5 Hz)
- 🎚️ Wavelength slider (0.5 – 3 units)
- 🎚️ Amplitude slider (0.3 – 1.5 units)
- 📐 Live display of the wave equation: `c = f × λ`
- ⏸️ Pause / Play toggle

---

### 3. 🔄 Wave Propagation Modes
Four selectable modes illustrating how EM waves behave under different physical conditions:

| Mode | Description |
|---|---|
| 🌌 Vacuum | Wave propagates at c = 3×10⁸ m/s with no attenuation |
| 🔷 Dense Medium | Wavelength compresses in glass (n = 1.5); frequency unchanged |
| 🪞 Reflection | Incident and reflected waves superpose at a perfect conductor |
| 💧 Refraction | Wave bends at a media boundary — Snell's Law: n₁sinθ₁ = n₂sinθ₂ |

---

### 4. 📡 Real-Life Applications
Six expandable cards connecting EM wave theory to technologies used in everyday life:

- 🛰️ **Satellite Communication** — GPS, broadband, broadcasting
- 📶 **Wi-Fi & 5G Networks** — microwave band wireless data transmission
- 🩻 **Medical X-Ray Imaging** — differential tissue absorption for diagnosis
- ☀️ **Solar Radiation** — photovoltaic energy conversion and climate
- 📱 **Mobile Networks** — 4G LTE and 5G beamforming antenna systems
- 🔬 **Spectroscopy & MRI** — atomic fingerprinting and soft tissue imaging

---

### 5. 🧲 Maxwell's Discovery — Historical Timeline
A chronological account of the development of electromagnetic theory, from its mathematical origins to experimental confirmation and modern impact.

- 👶 **1831** — Birth of James Clerk Maxwell in Edinburgh, Scotland
- 📚 **1855** — Mathematical formulation of Faraday's field concepts at Cambridge
- 💡 **1861** — Introduction of the displacement current term in Ampere's Law
- 📄 **1864** — Publication of the four Maxwell equations unifying electricity, magnetism, and light
- ⚡ **1887** — Experimental confirmation by Heinrich Hertz using a spark-gap transmitter
- 🌍 **Present** — Foundation of every modern wireless and photonic technology

Maxwell's four equations, as presented in the simulation:

```
∇·E  =  ρ/ε₀
∇·B  =  0
∇×E  =  −∂B/∂t
∇×B  =  μ₀J + μ₀ε₀ ∂E/∂t
```
